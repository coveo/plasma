# About Content

*Content*, also known as *copy*, refers to the text and information that's displayed in the UI. It's an integral part of a design system and can make or break the user's experience. You can't have content without design, and you can't have design without content.

The *content guidelines* define the writing standards and best practices for Coveo's Administration Console UI. Use them to write consistently, maintain a unified voice across the product, and apply the right tone for any UI context.

## Who is this for?

This style guide is intended for anyone who writes, edits, or reviews UX copy, including:

- Designers
- Tech writers
- Developers
- Other Product team members
- AI agents

## What you'll find

- **Fundamentals**: Core principles that apply across all UX copy.
    - **[Target audience](target-audience.md)**: An analysis of the audience including two end-user personas
    - **[Voice principles](voice-principles.md)**: The tone and personality behind all UX copy
    - **[Product terminology](product-terminology.md)**: Approved product terms and naming conventions
    - **[Writing mechanics](writing-mechanics.md)**: Grammar, structure, punctuation, spelling, and length guidelines

- **Guidelines**: Standard writing conventions for UI elements, organized by component.
    - **Call-to-action text**: *[buttons](../guidelines/call-to-action-text/buttons.md)*, links, breadcrumbs, filters, facets, and tags
    - **Form text**: *[labels](../guidelines/form-text/labels.md)*, inputs, placeholders, validation, and helper text
    - **Feedback text**: *[alerts](../guidelines/feedback-text/alerts.md)*, error messages, toasts, empty states, loading states, tooltips, and modals
    - **Data display text**: *[cards](../guidelines/data-display-text/cards.md)*, tables, metrics, badges, pills, and status tokens
    - **Layout and page text**: *[page titles](../guidelines/layout-page-text/page-titles.md)*, page descriptions, tabs, and collapsible panels
