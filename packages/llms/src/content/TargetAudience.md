> **Important for agents:** The personas below are representative archetypes. Do not use their names, quotes, or personal details in any UI copy. Use this page to understand writing priorities and adjust your copy accordingly.

Always write for the least technically experienced user in the audience. Copy must make the next step obvious, use plain language, and let users act without needing to learn the product first.

## Writing priorities by user type

### Nadia — Technical Platform Admin

Nadia is technically capable but needs context to interpret errors and outcomes. She's often slowed down by unclear steps or permission issues and needs to self-serve without opening a support ticket.

**When writing for Nadia:**

- Lead with what broke and what to do next
- Make error messages scannable — state the problem, then the fix
- Don't make her read to find the action — put it up front
- Avoid vague status messages like "An error occurred" — be specific

### Louise — Business Operations Owner

Louise is non-technical and intimidated by dense screens and jargon. She checks in on platform health and business impact but doesn't manage the platform directly.

**When writing for Louise:**

- Use plain language — no acronyms without explanation, no technical jargon
- Make status and outcomes immediately visible — don't bury them in detail
- Keep copy short and scannable
- Make navigation obvious — she shouldn't have to explore to find what she needs

## Default rule

When in doubt, write for Louise. If the copy is clear enough for a non-technical user, it is clear enough for everyone.
