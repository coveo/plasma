# Target Audience

The first step to writing great UX copy is understanding the customers who read it. At Coveo, that means writing for end-users who could be technical or non-technical, experts of the product or brand new to it.

The goal is to tailor the UX writing to the *least-experienced* members of the audience. Copy should make the next step obvious, use plain language, and help all users act without having to learn the product first.

## User personas

### Nadia, the Technical Platform Admin

![Corporate headshot of woman "Nadia"](../content-images/nadia-user-persona.png)
*"Just tell me what broke, where, and what to do next."*

| Role & responsibilities | Skills | Struggles | Needs & wants |
|--------------------------|--------|-----------|---------------|
| • Owns implementation setup and ongoing console maintenance<br>• Configures, validates, and troubleshoots the platform<br>• Works across indexing, permissions, and operational fixes<br>• Often bridges between client teams and PS/Support | • Technically comfortable<br>• Can follow complex workflows<br>• Strong at diagnosing issues and keeping things moving | • Finds the UI and workflows harder than they should be<br>• Needs more context to interpret errors and outcomes<br>• Friction from permissions, unclear steps, and manual workarounds slows her down | • Clear step-by-step guidance<br>• Actionable error messages<br>• Better diagnostics and faster navigation<br>• Wants to self-serve without opening a support loop every time |

### Louise, the Business Operations Owner

![Corporate headshot of woman "Louise"](../content-images/louise-user-persona.png)
*"I need to know whether this is worthwhile without becoming a platform expert."*

| Role & responsibilities | Skills | Struggles | Needs & wants |
|--------------------------|--------|-----------|---------------|
| • Coordinates stakeholders during buying, implementation, and ongoing operations<br>• Tracks adoption, value, and ROI<br>• Uses the console to check progress, status, and business impact<br>• Needs to keep the experience aligned across teams and phases | • Cross-functional communicator<br>• Good at prioritizing and translating business goals<br>• Comfortable with dashboards and high-level decision-making | • Technical language and dense screens are intimidating<br>• Hard to see whether the platform is healthy or delivering value<br>• Prefers not to rely on manual tracking or ask specialists for every answer | • Plain-language copy<br>• Clear status and value signals<br>• Role-specific views<br>• Wants confidence that the system is working and worth the effort |
