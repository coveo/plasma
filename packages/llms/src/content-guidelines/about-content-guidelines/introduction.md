# About Content Guidelines

This style guide defines the writing standards and content patterns for Coveo's administration console UX. Use it to write consistently, maintain a unified voice across the product, and apply the right tone for any UI context.

## Who is it for?

Content guidelines are intended for anyone who writes, edits, or reviews UX copy. This includes:

- Designers
- Tech writers
- Developers
- Other Product team members
- AI agents

## What you'll find

- **Target audience**: An analysis of the UX target audience and end-user personas to illustrate who reads the copy
- **Voice principles**: The tone and personality behind all UX copy
- **Product terminology**: Approved product terms and naming conventions
- **Writing mechanics**: Grammar, structure, punctuation, spelling, and length guidelines
- **Content patterns** — Standard writing conventions for components, organized by UI pattern type:
  - *Call-to-action text* — Buttons, links, breadcrumbs, filters, facets, and tags
  - *Form text* — Labels, inputs, placeholders, validation, and helper text
  - *Feedback text* — Alerts, error messages, toasts, empty states, loading states, tooltips, and modals
  - *Data display text* — Cards, tables, metrics, badges, pills, and status tokens
  - *Layout and page text* — Page titles, page descriptions, tabs, and collapsible panels
