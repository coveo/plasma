# Target Audience

The first step to writing great UX copy is understanding the users who read it.
At Coveo, that means writing for end users who could be technical or non-technical—experts of the administration console or brand new to it.

The goal is to tailor the UX writing to the *least experienced* members of the audience.
Copy should make the next step **obvious**, use **plain language**, and help all users act **without having to learn the product first**.

## User personas

### Louise, the Business Operations Owner

![Corporate headshot of woman in blazer](../images/louise-user-persona.png)
*"I need to know whether this is worthwhile without becoming a platform expert."*

| Role & Responsibilities | Skills | Struggles | Needs & Wants |
|-------------------------|--------|-----------|---------------|
| • Coordinates stakeholders during buying, implementation, and ongoing operations<br>• Tracks adoption, value, and ROI<br>• Uses the console to check progress, status, and business impact<br>• Needs to keep the experience aligned across teams and phases | • Cross-functional communicator<br>• Good at prioritizing and translating business goals<br>• Comfortable with dashboards and high-level decision-making | • Technical language and dense screens are intimidating<br>• Hard to see whether the platform is healthy or delivering value<br>• Prefers not to rely on manual tracking or ask specialists for every answer | • Plain-language copy<br>• Clear status and value signals<br>• Knowing where she needs to go on the page<br>• Wants confidence that the system is working and worth the effort |

### Nadia, the Technical Platform Admin

![Corporate headshot of woman with glasses](../images/nadia-user-persona.png)
*"Just tell me what broke, where, and what to do next."*

| Role & Responsibilities | Skills | Struggles | Needs & Wants |
|-------------------------|--------|-----------|---------------|
| • Owns implementation setup and ongoing console maintenance<br>• Configures, validates, and troubleshoots the platform<br>• Works across indexing, permissions, and operational fixes<br>• Often bridges between client teams and PS/Support | • Technically comfortable<br>• Can follow complex workflows<br>• Strong at diagnosing issues and keeping things moving | • Finds the UI and workflows harder than they should be<br>• Needs more context to interpret errors and outcomes<br>• Friction from permissions, unclear steps, and manual workarounds slows her down | • Copy that is helpful, but discreet, that doesn't slow her down<br>• Error messages with next steps<br>• Faster navigation<br>• Wants to self-serve without support |
